Let's learn about the different type of skin

"Before choosing the right products, it's important to know your skin type which are based on genetics and how your skin naturally behaves. Let’s start by understanding the different skin types and what they need."



OILY SKIN



  • Your skin looks shiny or greasy throughout the day, especially in the T-zone.
  • You often feel the need to blot your face or use oil-absorbing sheets.
  • Your pores appear large and more visible than usual.
  • Your makeup tends to melt, slide off, or fade quickly.
  • You frequently experience acne, blackheads, or whiteheads.

DRY SKIN



  • Your skin feels tight, especially after washing.
  • You notice flaking, rough patches, or peeling.
  • Your skin often looks dull or ashy.
  • You experience itchiness or irritation, especially in cold or dry weather.
  • Fine lines may appear more visible due to lack of moisture.

COMBINATION SKIN



  • Your T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) tends to be oily and shiny.
  • Your cheeks and other areas often feel dry or normal.
  • You experience occasional breakouts in oily areas but dryness elsewhere.
  • Your skin needs both oil-control and hydration in different zones.
  • Your pores may be enlarged in the oily zones but normal or small elsewhere.

SENSETIVE SKIN



  • Your skin easily reacts with redness, burning, or stinging sensations.
  • You often feel discomfort when using new skincare or cosmetic products.
  • Your skin becomes irritated by environmental factors like sun, wind, or cold.
  • You may experience itching, dryness, or small bumps without a clear cause.
  • Your skin looks blotchy or inflamed after cleansing or exfoliating.

NORMAL SKIN



  • Your skin feels balanced — not too oily or too dry.
  • You have a smooth texture with minimal blemishes or imperfections.
  • Your pores are small and not very visible.
  • Your skin has a healthy, radiant glow.
  • Your skin rarely feels sensitive or irritated.

"Not sure what your skin wants? Don’t worry — we’ve broken it down with easy solutions for every skin type."

Oily Skin

  • CLEANSER: Gel-based or foaming cleanser with salicylic acid or tea tree oil.
  • SERUM: Niacinamide serum (helps reduce oil and shrink pores).
  • MOISTURIZER: Lightweight, oil-free, gel-based moisturizers with hyaluronic acid.
  • SUNSCREEN: Matte-finish, gel or water-based SPF (non-comedogenic).
  • DIET TIP: Reduce fried and sugary foods; include more green veggies, zinc-rich foods, and drink plenty of water.

DRY SKIN

  • CLEANSER: Cream or milk-based cleansers that don’t strip natural oils.
  • SERUM: Hyaluronic acid or squalane serum for intense hydration.
  • MOISTURIZER: Rich, cream-based moisturizers with ceramides or shea butter.
  • SUNSCREEN: Hydrating SPF with moisturizing ingredients (like glycerin or aloe vera).
  • DIET TIP: Eat healthy fats (avocados, nuts, seeds), and stay well-hydrated. Omega-3s are especially helpful.

COMBINATION SKIN

  • CLEANSER: Gentle gel cleanser that balances oil and hydration.
  • SERUM: Niacinamide + hyaluronic acid (multi-benefit for T-zone and dry areas).
  • MOISTURIZER Lightweight lotion or gel-cream, or use different products for different zones.
  • SUNSCREEN: Lightweight, non-greasy SPF that doesn’t clog pores.
  • DIET TIP: Maintain a balanced diet. Include probiotics (like yogurt), fiber, and hydrate well.

SENSETIVE SKIN

  • CLEANSER: Fragrance-free, gentle cream cleansers with calming ingredients (like oat or chamomile).
  • SERUM: Centella Asiatica or calendula-based serum (calms inflammation).
  • MOISTURIZER: Barrier-repair creams with ceramides and minimal ingredients.
  • SUNSCREEN: Mineral/physical sunscreen (zinc oxide or titanium dioxide-based).
  • DIET TIP: Avoid spicy or highly processed foods. Include anti-inflammatory foods like turmeric, berries, and green tea.

NORMAL SKIN

  • CLEANSER: Gentle foaming or gel cleanser for daily refresh.
  • SERUM: Vitamin C or hyaluronic acid serum to maintain glow and hydration.
  • MOISTURIZER: Light cream or lotion to keep skin balanced.
  • SUNSCREEN: Lightweight broad-spectrum SPF (cream or gel depending on climate).
  • DIET TIP: Continue a balanced diet rich in fruits, veggies, and water to maintain skin health.
